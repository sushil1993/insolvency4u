Insolvency FAQs

Quick answers to common questions, plus links to deeper guides.

What should I do if my business is failing?

Speak to a specialist early. We’ll assess duties, stabilise the situation and set a safe plan. Delay narrows options and can increase personal risk.

I have HMRC arrears (VAT/PAYE/CT). Will I lose my home?

Personal homes are usually protected unless there are personal guarantees or wrongful trading issues. We explain your position clearly and help you avoid mistakes.

Can I liquidate with a Bounce Back Loan outstanding?

Yes. Bounce Back Loans are company debts. We’ll make sure the process is handled correctly and your duties are met.

Do I have to keep trading while insolvent?

No — you must avoid worsening the position of creditors. We advise on when to stop trading and how to do that properly.

What happens to employees in a CVL?

Employees are usually made redundant and can claim certain entitlements from the Redundancy Payments Service. We guide you through notifications and paperwork.

How long does a creditors’ voluntary liquidation take?

Relief starts at appointment; the overall case can run 6–12 months depending on complexity. We keep you informed throughout.

Are your consultation fees deducted if I proceed?

Yes. The £395 consultation is credited against our full service if you go ahead.

What documents will you need from me?

Basic accounts, bank statements, creditor list, assets, liabilities, any leases and guarantees. We’ll give you a simple checklist.

Can I start a new company after liquidation?

Often yes, with restrictions. We explain name reuse rules, directorship considerations and how to restart safely.

How do I choose an Insolvency Practitioner?

Experience, responsiveness and clarity matter. We prepare your case and work with trusted IPs so the process is smooth.
