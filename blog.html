When does liquidation start?

Liquidation begins when directors or creditors formally resolve to wind up the company and an Insolvency Practitioner is appointed.

Before that point there’s opportunity to plan: gather records, stop taking new credit, and protect your position as a director.

We walk you through the timeline so there are no surprises, and we prepare the case so it moves smoothly.

Is liquidation the same as insolvency?

No. Insolvency is when the company can’t pay its debts as they fall due or liabilities exceed assets.

Liquidation is one possible process used to close an insolvent company in an orderly, legal way.

Understanding the difference helps you choose between rescue and closure at the right time.

Are liquidation fees tax deductible?

Liquidation costs are company expenses. The treatment depends on the company’s circumstances and tax position.

We explain how fees are handled, what is paid when, and how to avoid unnecessary costs.

The earlier you speak to us, the more we can control the overall cost of closure.

Why voluntary liquidation (CVL)?

A CVL lets directors take control and close the business in an orderly way rather than waiting for a creditor to force action.

It reduces stress, protects directors, and usually results in a smoother, faster outcome for all parties.

We prepare everything in advance so the liquidator can proceed without delay.

How is the price of liquidation calculated?

Fees reflect complexity: number of creditors, employee issues, asset realisations, investigations and reporting obligations.

We scope your case up front so you know the range before you decide.

Our aim is clarity and fairness — no surprises.

How to pick a good liquidator

Look for track record, responsiveness, and clear explanations in plain English.

You should understand timelines, responsibilities, and reporting before you sign.

We work with experienced IPs and prepare your case so the process is efficient.

My husband’s business is failing — what can I do?

You can help by encouraging an early, calm conversation with a specialist. Delay narrows options and adds stress.
